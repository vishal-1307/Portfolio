About Me

I also started Mithila Kritikala, an NGO where we work on keeping Mithila’s art and culture alive while teaching practical skills like Mithila Painting & Stitching. Along with running it, I also designed and developed the website myself. That project gave me real hands-on experience and made me realize how powerful the web is for connecting people and showcasing creativity.

Right now, my main goal is to keep improving my skills and work on projects that actually make a difference. In the future, I also want to dive into Cybersecurity because that’s a field I’m really curious about.

Mithila Kritikala NGO Website

This project is incredibly close to my heart as it brings to life the vision of Mithila Kritikala—an NGO dedicated to preserving the precious heritage of Mithila painting and sewing, while empowering rural women with skills for a sustainable livelihood.
I designed and developed this website to serve as the digital home for our mission. It acts as a global platform to showcase the intricate beauty of the art form and to share the powerful stories of the artisans behind it. Key features include an elegant gallery to display the artwork, detailed artist profiles that give a voice to our artisans, and a secure donation system to enable supporters to contribute to our cause.
Built from the ground up using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, the site is crafted to be visually appealing and highly user-friendly. For me, this is more than just a project; it's a bridge between tradition and technology, built to empower a community and keep a beautiful cultural legacy alive.

Student Information System

As a hands-on initiative to apply and solidify my SQL knowledge, I developed this Student Information System project. The primary objective was to design and implement a robust and efficient relational database from the ground up to manage the core data of an educational institution.
The project involved creating a logical schema with interconnected tables for students, courses, and enrollments. I authored a variety of SQL queries to perform essential CRUD (Create, Read, Update, Delete) operations, effectively managing the flow of information within the system. This practical exercise was instrumental in deepening my understanding of data modeling, schema design, and the art of writing effective queries.
This project stands as a testament to my strong grasp of database management fundamentals and serves as a foundational milestone in my journey of building data-centric applications.
